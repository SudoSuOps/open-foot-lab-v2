Why it matters

Risk comes from many places. The problem is the same.

Diabetes Poor circulation Neuropathy Surgery Prior wounds Amputation Deformity Pressure Aging Mobility challenges

Small changes can become serious when they are missed.

What you track

Document the foot over time

OpenFootLab helps people and caregivers keep a living record — and turns it into something a clinician can read in minutes.

Photos over time

A visual history of the foot so change is obvious side by side.

Notes & daily checks

Quick daily entries build a habit and a timeline of how the foot feels.

Footwear records

What was worn, and when — footwear is often the hidden variable.

Offloading history

Pads, inserts, and devices used — what worked and what didn't.

Wound history

Track sites, healing, and recurrence in one continuous record.

Temperature observations

Warmth can precede a problem — log it and watch the trend.

Clinician-ready timelines

Everything above assembles into a clear, chronological summary — so a clinician sees the whole trajectory of the foot at a glance, not a scramble of loose photos and memories.
